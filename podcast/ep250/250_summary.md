**Ep. 250 — Bittersweet Beginnings**
*Fragmented Podcast · Don Felker, Kaushik Gopal · 34 min · 2024-10-29*

Don Felker uses episode 250 as his farewell from Fragmented, tracing a career arc where Android was never the destination — always the vehicle. Starting with a T-Mobile G1 in 2008 while on the bench at a consulting firm, he stumbled into soundboard apps that hit 20 million installs and generated unexpected ad revenue. That success opened doors: a book deal (Android App Development for Dummies), a fully remote job at early-stage Groupon, consulting gigs at MyFitnessPal and others, and eventually the podcast itself in 2015. Each step wasn't driven by passion for Android specifically — it was driven by pre-existing goals (write a book, speak publicly, work remotely, run a business) that Android happened to unlock.

The through-line Don keeps returning to: Android is a tool in a toolbox, the same as Rails, .NET, or Golang. When he ran out of Android-specific goals to pursue — books written, business built, podcast done — the vehicle had served its purpose. He's still writing Android code (Jumpstart Android, a personal fitness timer app), but he's stepping away from the show to chase whatever comes next without the Android-specific framing.

Kaushik confirms Fragmented will continue and evolve under his stewardship, hinting at a gap he sees in the current Android content landscape — lots of tutorials and YouTube, but something still missing from the conversation.

**Why it's worth your time:** If you've ever felt like your current tech stack is a means to an end rather than an identity, Don's framing of Android as a "vehicle" is a useful mental model — and his origin story is a good reminder that most career pivots look inevitable only in hindsight.
