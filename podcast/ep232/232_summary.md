**Ep. 232 — Frustration, Flow State and "The Madness"**
*Fragmented Podcast · Don Felker & Kaushik Gopal · 44 min · 2022-06-13*

Don and Kaushik riff on a shared developer experience: burning hours on a rabbit hole you know you should abandon, but can't. Don's morning lost to a CSS framework upgrade (that he never finished) kicks off an honest conversation about the gap between knowing good time management and actually practicing it when you're deep in a problem.

Both hosts distinguish between regular flow state and what Don calls "the madness" — a near-manic hyper-focus where you're either flying or chipping away obsessively, but either way the clock disappears. The dark side isn't just lost time; it's the background process that runs for hours after you stop, replaying possible solutions while you're eating lunch or in a meeting. Don frames the upside as a "pull forward" — the unresolved problem creates momentum into the next session, if you have the time to return to it.

On tactics: Kaushik advocates for strict timeboxing (shoutout to a college classmate who learned it at Pivotal Labs doing XP) and running back-to-back 25-minute Pomodoro cycles not to stop working, but to stay conscious of how many blocks you're burning. Don's friend takes it further — planning the entire week in Pomodoros on Sunday, capping himself at 6–10 blocks per day and expecting to retrue the plan by Wednesday. Don admits he followed the system for six months with mixed results. Neither host has a clean answer; the episode is more diagnosis than prescription.

The most honest moment: Don knew Option B worked in 10 minutes, switched back to Option A anyway because it "beat him," burned another 90 minutes, and only stopped by closing the IDE and walking away from the computer entirely.

**Why it's worth your time:** If you've ever lost a morning to something that wasn't on your plan and couldn't explain why, this episode names the feeling precisely. No silver bullets, but the timeboxing and Pomodoro discussion is concrete enough to try Monday morning.
