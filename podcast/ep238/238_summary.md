**Ep. 238 — How to Land a Job (if you got laid off or if you're just starting out)**
*Fragmented · Don Felker · 27 min · 2022-11-21*

Don Felker shares the exact job-search playbook he's used for 20 years — through recessions, layoffs, and consulting gaps — claiming he's never gone without a position for more than 24–48 hours.

The core four steps, in order: (1) Update your LinkedIn profile and build your resume using LinkedIn's resume builder. (2) Toggle "Open to Work" — recruiters will find you fast. (3) Dig through your old email and LinkedIn messages, tag every recruiter you've ever interacted with, and blast them a short templated message saying you're back on the market with your current skills and preferences. (4) Manual discovery — hit company career pages and job boards directly, and lean on warm referrals inside target companies whenever possible.

The recruiter angle is the episode's main thesis. Don treats recruiters as a strategic asset rather than spam to block: accept every connection, reply with a canned "I'm booked for 3–6 months, check back then" message, label them, and archive. When you need work, that tagged inbox becomes a pipeline you can activate in hours. For boosting visibility further, he recommends writing about the tech you want to work in (Medium, WordPress, anywhere), building and open-sourcing a demo app on GitHub, and optionally shipping it to an app store.

**Why it's worth your time:** If you've been at one company for years and haven't interviewed recently, this episode gives you a concrete, no-fluff checklist to start executing immediately — especially the recruiter tagging system, which is genuinely underused by most developers.
