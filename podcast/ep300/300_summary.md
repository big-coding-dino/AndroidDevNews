**Ep. 300 — From Vibe Coding to Software Engineering**
*Fragmented Podcast · Kaushik & Yuri Souza · 9 min · Jan 12, 2026*

Fragmented is pivoting from Android to AI — but not the hype-driven kind. Kaushik's thesis: vibe coding works for prototypes, but nobody is seriously addressing how working engineers use AI on large, legacy, team-maintained codebases. Questions like "how do we avoid unreviewable diffs?", "which tasks should AI touch?", and "how do we keep critical paths deterministic?" aren't getting answered publicly in ways that help people actually ship software.

New co-host Yuri Souza joins with a sharp analogy: most AI content sounds like high schoolers bragging about sex — loud claims that fall apart under scrutiny. The show's goal is to bridge vibe coding and real engineering: not AI replacing your work, but AI amplifying your capacity to ship quality code.

**Why it's worth your time:** If you're tired of AI content that's either breathless hype or useless to your day job, this pivot episode signals a show trying to occupy the gap. Worth subscribing to see if they deliver on it.
