**Ep. 241 — The Pressure to Ship: Fear, Uncertainty and Doubt**
*Fragmented · Don Felker & Kaushik Gopal · 45 min · Jan 10, 2023*

Don Felker, with 20+ years of Android development experience, describes hitting a wall where spending weeks deep in legacy codebases — making 30-line changes across a full day — made him genuinely question whether he could still build anything end-to-end. The doubt wasn't about knowing *how* things work (auth, databases, architecture) but whether he could still assemble them into a shipping product at speed.

To test himself, he built a complete web app from concept to live in 24 hours. The exercise was less about the technology and more about proving to himself that the builder instinct hadn't atrophied — just the muscle. The bigger realization: he'd been measuring himself against the wrong benchmarks. Library authors, compiler engineers, animation specialists — they're a different type. Don identifies as a *builder*: someone who uses software the way others use wood or metal, most at home moving fast across the full stack at an early-stage company.

Kaushik mirrors this, placing himself in a different bucket — the refactoring/structure specialist who thrives when an organically-grown codebase needs a harness and a strategy. Both acknowledge these buckets shift over time and that the industry needs all of them.

**Why it's worth your time:** If you've ever felt like a fraud after a week of 40-line diffs, this episode is a direct hit. The tactical reframe — build something small and complete to reset your confidence — is immediately actionable, and the "what kind of engineer am I?" framework is worth sitting with.
