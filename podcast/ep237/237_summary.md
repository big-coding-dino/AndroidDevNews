**Ep. 237 — Compose vs XML – Which one should you learn as an Android Dev?**
*Fragmented Podcast · Don Felker & Kaushik Gopal · 40 min · 2022-11-14*

Don and Kaushik debate whether new Android developers should learn Jetpack Compose, XML layouts, or both — and they don't fully agree, at least not at first.

Kaushik opens with a nuance: the right answer depends on experience level. For someone with 3+ years of Android, XML knowledge still matters. For a greenfield learner? Go straight to Compose. His evidence: new Android engineers he's interviewed have never touched RxJava and are coroutines-only — the same generational shift is happening with Compose. The KMM starter project from JetBrains now defaults to Compose without comment, which he reads as a signal that the conversation is closing.

Don comes from a contractor/consultant angle and starts more conservative — he'd recommend learning XML first because virtually every existing codebase he's touched is still XML-only. But he draws a sharp line: if you're a full-time employee steering a team, staying on XML long-term is a mistake. His argument is developer happiness: forcing engineers onto old tech they didn't train on drives turnover, kills velocity, and tanks morale. He points to his own Jumpstart Android project as proof of conviction — he started it fresh in Compose because of his React and Flutter background, and the declarative model clicked immediately.

Both converge on a historical parallel: Google backing Kotlin over Java ended that debate regardless of preference, and the same dynamic is playing out with Compose vs XML. The question isn't really whether Compose wins — it's when, and how fast your team needs to get there.

**Why it's worth your time:** If you're an Android dev weighing where to invest, or a tech lead deciding when to push your team toward Compose, this episode gives you the practical framing — consulting context vs. full-time employment, junior vs. senior, greenfield vs. legacy — to make the call with confidence rather than just following the hype.
